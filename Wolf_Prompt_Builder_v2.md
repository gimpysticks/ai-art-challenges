# Wolf Prompt Builder v2
## Wolf-First Constraints & Instructions

### Purpose

This instruction set is designed to create images that are immediately recognizable as wolves by wolf-focused communities.

The wolf must always be the primary subject.

If a viewer cannot identify the creature as a wolf within one second, the prompt has failed.

---

# Core Philosophy

Wolf First.

Everything else is secondary.

Priority order:

1. Wolf anatomy
2. Wolf presence
3. Environment
4. Atmosphere
5. Symbolism

Never reverse this order.

---

# Subject Hierarchy

The wolf must occupy most of the visual attention.

The wolf should be:

- Ancient
- Powerful
- Intelligent
- Wild
- Mythic
- Dominant

Avoid making the wolf feel like a costumed character.

Avoid making the wolf overly human.

Avoid turning the wolf into a symbolic object.

# Wolf Anatomy Requirements

Prompts should emphasize:

- Powerful wolf head
- Strong muzzle
- Intelligent eyes
- Thick neck fur
- Layered fur textures
- Broad shoulders
- Powerful forelegs
- Large paws
- Dense winter coat
- Scarred muzzle
- Weathered fur
- Alert ears
- Natural posture
- Realistic musculature

The wolf should feel physically believable.

---

# Wolf Anatomy Weighting

Target composition:

80% Wolf Anatomy

15% Environment

5% Atmosphere and Symbolism

Never allow symbolism to dominate the wolf.

# Things To Avoid

## Humanized Elements

Avoid:

- Human clothing
- Human armor
- Human expressions
- Human poses
- Human accessories
- Character costume design

## Religious Elements

Avoid:

- Saint imagery
- Rosaries
- Reliquaries
- Prayer banners
- Monk robes
- Ecclesiastical garments
- Floating halos
- Cathedral symbolism

## Overused Symbolism

Avoid:

- Excessive moon symbolism
- Decorative crowns
- Floating symbols
- Magical architecture
- Complex ceremonial objects

## Anatomical Distortions

Avoid:

- Werewolf anatomy
- Human hands
- Human posture
- Excessive fantasy mutations
- Cartoon proportions

# Composition

Preferred:

- Portrait orientation
- Three-quarter view
- Full wolf portrait
- Dominant central subject
- Strong silhouette

Avoid clutter.

Avoid visual competition.

The wolf should own the frame.
